Phase 1: Foundation Building

Foundation building refers to the initial steps in interview preparation process. Foundation Building in interview preparation refers to the process of establishing a strong and comprehensive understanding of the basics and fundamentals related to the job you're applying for.

1. Self-Assessment

  • Identify your strengths, weaknesses, skills, and experiences.
  • Reflect on your career goals and aspirations.

2. Research the Company

  • Understand the company's mission, values, products/services, and recent news.
  • Research the role you're applying for, including responsibilities and requirements.

3. Prepare Your Resume and Cover Letter

A Resume is a concise document summarizing your skills , experience , and education to showcase your qualifications for a job.

Many people confuse Resume with CV and viceversa

Both Resume and CV (Curriculum Vitae) are documents used to showcase your qualifications for a job. While they serve a similar purpose, there are some key differences:
Feature Resume CV
Purpose A snapshot of your most relevant qualifications for a specific job. A comprehensive overview of your academic and professional history.
Length Generally limited to one or two pages. Can be multiple pages long, especially for academic or research positions.
Focus Highlights recent experiences and emphasizes your most relevant skills and accomplishments. Provides a chronological overview of your entire career history.

While cover letter is a brief persuasive document that accompanies your resume,introducing yourself to a potential employer and expressing your strong interest in the specific position and company
